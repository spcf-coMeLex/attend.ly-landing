Why Attend.ly?

Blockchain-Powered Security

With Attendly, every attendance record is stored securely on the ICP blockchain, ensuring data integrity and preventing tampering. This level of security is unparalleled in traditional attendance systems.

Attendance Analytics

Get a clear view of each student's attendance history. Use these insights to provide targeted support where it's needed most and to recognize and reward student efforts seamlessly.

Real-Time Notifications for Parents

Stay updated with your child's school attendance through immediate email notifications. Whether your child is present, late, or absent, you'll know as soon as they check into class.

Gamified Rewards System

We believe in motivating students not just to attend but to engage. Attendly introduces a unique rewards system where students earn points for attendance, participation, and school community involvement. These points can then be redeemed for educational rewards.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Where can I download the app?

    Attend.ly is currently available to a select group of users through Expo Go, which allows for easy testing and feedback on iOS and Android devices. You can sign up for testing below. Additionally, Android users can test the app via APK. Stay tuned for our wider release!

  • Is an Internet Identity account required to use Attend.ly?

    Yes, to ensure the highest level of security and leverage the benefits of blockchain technology with the ICP blockchain, an Internet Identity account is necessary for all users. The Internet Identity is an anonymizing blockchain authentication system on the Internet Computer that allows users to sign in securely and pseudonymously to dapps, without the need for passwords or personal identifying information.

  • Why use ICP blockchain for attendance monitoring?

    The Internet Computer Protocol (ICP) is a unique, general-purpose blockchain that hosts smart contracts, known as canisters. It's designed to act as a World Computer, replacing traditional IT and hosting a new generation of Web 3.0 services and applications that run solely from the blockchain, without the need for traditional IT. ICP blockchain offers unmatched security, scalability, and innovation. By using ICP, Attend.ly ensures that attendance data is securely stored and remains immutable, setting a new standard in educational technology.

  • What sets Attend.ly apart from traditional attendance systems?

    Attend.ly distinguishes itself by offering a secure, tamper-proof method to record and store attendance data on the blockchain. Unlike traditional systems, Attend.ly rewards academic and extracurricular participation with points, merging the benefits of blockchain and cryptocurrency technologies with educational achievements.

  • What can points earned with Attend.ly be redeemed for?

    The points earned through Attend.ly can be redeemed for academic incentives, such as bonus points on quizzes or exemptions from exams. This system is designed to ensure that rewards are educationally relevant and do not impose additional financial burdens on the institutions.

  • Can points be transferred to a crypto wallet?

    We are actively exploring the integration of Attend.ly points with cryptocurrency wallets. This feature is in development, and updates will be provided as we progress.

  • How long until the points expire?

    The expiration of points is designed with the student's academic journey in mind. Ideally, points would remain valid throughout the student's tenure at the institution, encouraging ongoing engagement and participation. Specific terms and conditions regarding point expiration will be clearly communicated to all users.

  • Will Attend.ly remain free?

    We are currently testing the waters for the pricing model, but Attend.ly will introduce a tiered pricing model, which will be based on the number of teachers and students utilizing the app. For instance, a basic plan may accommodate 1 teacher and as many as 30 students. We also plan to offer premium features for paying users, which will be detailed at a later stage.
